## Executive Summary

The AITourPilot Spring 2025 LinkedIn campaign invested approximately **EUR 400** across paid ads and generated **70+ mailing list sign-ups**, **21,000+ reach**, and a **21% demo email conversion rate** — all starting from just 3 followers on the company page.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Period** | February 24 – June 2025 |
| **Total Paid Spend** | ~EUR 400 |
| **Creative Concept** | AI-generated video clips of famous paintings coming alive |
| **Teasers Produced** | 3 (The Ninth Wave, Van Gogh, Vermeer) + 5-ad campaign |
| **Mailing List Sign-ups** | 70+ (42 still active across 15+ countries) |
| **Demo Email Conversion** | 21% |
| **Total Paid Reach** | 21,000+ unique accounts |
| **Total Video Views** | 6,000+ |
| **Starting Followers** | 3 |

### What Made It Work

1. **The "paintings come alive" concept** — AI-animated masterpieces stopped the scroll on LinkedIn. No one else was doing this in museum tech.
2. **Founder storytelling outperformed corporate posts** — Hermann's personal narrative (daughter stepping into paintings, childhood wonder) made the technology feel human.
3. **Dual-account strategy** — posting from both personal account (existing network) and company page (3 followers) extended reach.
4. **Smart audience targeting** — 150K museum professionals across US cities, UK, Nordics, Netherlands, and DACH.
5. **Engagement with critics built credibility** — thoughtful responses to Albertina and academic critics turned skeptics into trust-building moments.

### Side-by-Side Comparison

| Metric | T1 English | T1 German | T2 Van Gogh |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-------------|

| Duration | Feb 24-27 | Feb 25-28 | Mar 4-7 |

| Engagement Rate | **1.34%** | 1.19% | 0.71% |
| View Rate | **28.9%** | 27.3% | 25.47% |
| CPV | EUR 0.05 | EUR 0.08 | **EUR 0.04** |

| Landing Page Clicks | **9** | 6 | 2 |
| Video Views (2s+) | 2,066 | 1,265 | **2,648** |
| Video Completions | 50 | 34 | **313** |

### Key Findings

1. **Van Gogh (T2) won on reach efficiency** — nearly 2x the reach at the lowest CPM (EUR 9.62), likely due to Audience Expansion being enabled
2. **Ninth Wave (T1 EN) won on engagement quality** — highest CTR (1.25%), best engagement rate, most landing page clicks
3. **Van Gogh had dramatically better video completion** — 2.75% vs ~0.63%, driven by shorter format (12s vs 30s)
4. **German campaign underperformed across all metrics** — higher costs, lower engagement, zero LinkedIn page clicks
5. **Shorter videos = higher completion; longer videos = more clicks** — use 12-15s for awareness, 25-30s for conversion

## Post Style Analysis

### Personal Account Structure (Hermann Kudlich)

Every successful personal post followed this pattern:

1. **Hook** — Provocative question or emotional statement (1-2 lines)
2. **Personal story** — Emotional, visual, relatable (2-4 lines)
3. **The reveal** — What AITourPilot is doing about it (2-3 lines)
4. **Mission statement** — Why this matters (1-2 lines)
5. **Launch CTA** — Spring launch + dual links
6. **Hashtags** — 5-6 targeted tags

### Best Performing Hooks

> "What If a Painting Could Talk?"

> "Museums have always been about discovery — this just takes it one step further."

> "What secrets does Vermeer hide? The stories behind the stillness."

### Company Account Structure (AITourPilot)

1. **Provocative question** — Challenge assumptions about museum visits
2. **Vision** — What museum experiences could be
3. **Pain point** — What's wrong with current museum tech
4. **Solution positioning** — AITourPilot as conversation, not app
5. **Signature couplet** — Memorable one-liners
6. **Launch CTA** — Dual links + hashtags

### The Signature Lines (Brand Voice)

> "Your guide isn't an app. It's a conversation."
> "Your tour isn't scripted. It's your story."

These two lines appeared across multiple posts and became the campaign's defining copy.

### Tone Characteristics

| Personal Account | Company Account |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Emotionally vulnerable | Thought leadership |
| Father-founder narrative | Empathetic to visitor pain |
| Intellectual curiosity | Aspirational |
| Accessible technical language | Differentiation-focused |
| Conversational, not corporate | Poetic closing lines |

### What to Avoid (Learned from Drafts)

- **Too product-focused:** Feature lists don't work — lead with emotion
- **Too long:** Several drafts were rejected as "too long for LinkedIn"
- **Generic AI language:** "AI-powered" alone needs emotional context
- **Overselling:** Strategy docs explicitly warn against "in-your-face-selling"
- **"Passive museum visits":** This phrase was flagged as dismissive by museum professionals

## Engagement & Community Response

### The Critics

| Critic | Affiliation | Core Concern |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| **Marietta Mautner** | Albertina Museum | AI kills mystery, flattens interpretation, creates dependency |
| **Teresa Ferrero Munoz** | Museum professional | AI must complement, never replace the artwork |
| **Nicolas** | Museum professional | Dismissive of museum progress, environmental impact |
| **Paul** | University of Birmingham | AI creates laziness, human disconnect |

### The Proven Response Framework

Every successful response followed this 5-part structure:

1. **Acknowledge** — Thank the critic genuinely, validate their expertise
2. **Align** — Show you share their core value
3. **Reframe** — Redirect from "AI replaces" to "AI enables"
4. **Evidence** — Provide concrete design decisions
5. **Bridge** — End with gratitude and invitation to continue

### The Winning Line

> **"We want to revive curiosity, not resolve it."**

This single phrase became the rhetorical anchor — it directly addresses every critic's fear while positioning AITourPilot on the right side of the debate.

### Key Techniques

- **Validate first:** "I truly appreciate your passion..." disarms defensiveness
- **"Opposite goal" reframe:** "AITourPilot was created with the opposite goal in mind"
- **Audio-first differentiation:** "Unlike screens that pull attention away..."
- **Metaphors:** "Training wheels," "bridge not destination," "gateway"
- **Sign with title:** Adds authority, shows founder cares
- **Respond in critic's language:** Spanish reply to Teresa shows respect

### Messaging Rules Derived from Critiques

| Rule | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Never say "passive museum visits" | Dismissive of museums and their audiences |
| Never position AI as the center | The visitor and artwork must remain central |
| Anchor in emotional value, not tech | People fear tech; they crave connection |
| Imply respect for museums | These are potential partners, not problems to solve |
| Invite curiosity, not convenience | "Convenience" triggers dependency fears |
| Always clarify: audio guide, not AR/VR | Animated videos create a VR impression |

## Email Campaign Performance

### Demo Email (21% Conversion Rate)

The winning template:

> Subject: Thanks for your interest in AITourPilot – Let's book your demo
>
> Thank you for your interest in AITourPilot! I'm excited to show you how our conversational AI can transform the way people experience exhibitions and cultural spaces.
>
> I'd be happy to schedule a personalized 20-minute demo where we can:
> - Walk through real examples of AITourPilot in action
> - Discuss how it might fit your audience, context, or venue
> - Answer any questions around implementation and engagement

**Why it worked:** Short, respectful, specific (20 minutes), three clear bullets, no pressure.

### Personalized Outreach Pattern

Each email was customized with:
1. Research about their specific institution
2. Connection to their mission
3. Three consistent value bullets
4. Signature closing: *"AITourPilot is more than facts — it's about sparking emotion and curiosity when someone pauses in front of an object, a place, or a story that speaks to them."*

## Recommendations for Next Campaign

### Replicate

1. The "paintings come alive" video format — this is the creative moat
2. Personal founder storytelling alongside company posts
3. EUR 25/day, 4-day burst format
4. The "What if..." opening hook
5. The signature couplet (guide/conversation, tour/story)
6. Comment starters to seed engagement
7. Engagement prompt questions ("What would YOU ask [Artist]?")
8. Dual CTA (visitors + museum professionals)
9. Critique-response framework (Acknowledge → Align → Reframe → Evidence → Bridge)
10. Personalized demo emails with institution-specific research

### Improve

| Issue | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| Videos look like VR but product is audio | Clearer "audio guide" positioning in every post |
| Company page growth was slow (3→10) | Explicitly ask for follows in CTAs |
| No social proof in early posts | Include sign-up counts, museum interest, demo numbers |
| Posts felt disconnected | Number the series ("Teaser 3 of 5") |
| German campaign underperformed | Reduce DE budget or switch to OR-targeting |
