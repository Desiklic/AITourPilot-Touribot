1. Who We Are

At AITourPilot, we use AI to enhance museum experiences and revolutionize how people view, understand, and feel art. Our technology acts as an “art translator,” connecting dots and creating personalized experiences.

Ultimately, we are a companion who knows about art, recommends pieces, and explains them in a language you understand, tailored to your knowledge and perspective.

For the User
	•	Democratizing Art: We break the elitist view of art and make it accessible to everyone, regardless of age or cultural background. Tour Pilot is designed by and for the user, adapting to people.
	•	Telling Stories: Behind every painting and sculpture is a story—a “why.” Beyond materials and techniques, we help users understand the context of each creation, igniting their passion.
	•	No Crowds: We dissolve the masses that crowd around the most famous pieces. We create exclusive itineraries tailored to the visitor’s mood, leading to less crowded spots and hidden gems. We break the “must-see” mold and focus on the “must-discover.”

For the Museum
	•	Not Just Any AI: We index all museum documentation, understand visitor preferences with brief questions, and create custom tours.
	•	New Flows: Our tailored itineraries breathe life into museums, inviting visitors to explore beyond the usual.
	•	Valuable Data: Over time, we provide qualitative and quantitative insights about what visitors enjoy and find impactful, helping you rethink layouts and exhibits.
	•	Increased Demand: Personalizing the experience not only enhances visitor satisfaction but also boosts museum revenues.

2. Problems and Solutions

The Problem
	•	Lack of Visitor Insights: Museums lack sufficient data on visitor flows, preferences, behaviors, decision-making, and time spent at exhibits.
	•	Generic Guided Tours: Museum tours are often generic and fail to adapt to individual visitors’ knowledge, context, and interests, leading to impersonal experiences.
	•	Disconnection from Art: Many people feel disconnected from art and museums.
	•	Technological Barriers: Technology can be a hurdle for older visitors or those unfamiliar with complex interfaces.

Solutions
	•	AI-Personalized Guides: Real-time, personalized narratives attract new demographics to museums.
	•	Increased Loyalty Through Data: Data analytics enhance personalized visits, increasing visitor engagement and loyalty.
	•	Personal Connection: We make art relatable and exciting, sparking interest in younger audiences and those less familiar with museum culture.
	•	Simple Interfaces: Intuitive design reduces technological complexity for users and museums alike.

3. How AITourPilot Works

1. Getting Started

We work with two databases:
	1.	Knowledge Database: Includes all information about artwork, context, techniques, etc.
	2.	Creative Database: A vector database containing documentation, anecdotes, and analysis, creating tailored content based on the user.

Why two databases?
One provides information, and the other adapts it to the user. Addressing adults, children, and teenagers differently is crucial for creating the best experience and speaking a language accessible to everyone.

2. Museum and User Integration
	•	Museum: After indexing all data, we integrate the app as an option during online ticket purchases. We also create a landing page tailored to the museum’s branding to explain AITourPilot.
	•	User: Once logged in, users take a brief test to identify their interests, mood, and preferences. The adventure begins, with the AI adapting as they progress and asking simple questions to ensure the experience aligns with their expectations. After the visit, we ask for feedback to gauge satisfaction.

3. Visitor Experience
	•	Immersive Tours: Once the initial setup is complete, users enjoy a personalized, immersive experience tailored to their interests.
	•	Approachable AI: AITourPilot is not an art history professor—it’s a companion sharing anecdotes, interesting facts, and recommendations.

4. Understanding the User

With deeper insights into the user, we can learn:
	1.	Which exhibits are most popular.
	2.	General interests of the audience.
	3.	Opportunities for designing experiences to attract diverse user profiles.
	4.	Common visitor questions about the museum and exhibits.
	5.	A user map representing the museum audience.
	6.	Relevant keywords.
