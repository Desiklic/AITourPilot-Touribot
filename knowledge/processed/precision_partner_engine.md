## The Objective

### 60-Day Goal

Secure **1 pilot museum partnership** -- paid or strong revenue-share with defined conversion path -- publicly usable as a reference case.

**Target deal value:** EUR 45,000-75,000 (implementation) + EUR 24,000-42,000/year (license)

**Not the goal:** 10,000 leads, generic SaaS scaling, SDR simulation.

### Why Museum-First (Not Consumer-First)

A single museum deal is worth more than 1,000 passive sign-ups. Once you land one:

- Content credibility increases (real case study)
- Consumer growth accelerates (visitors discover the app organically)
- PR becomes possible (award + live museum = media story)
- Second museum is dramatically easier to close
- Funding conversations become viable

Consumer traction is a **supporting asset** that creates leverage, not the primary engine.

## Module 1: ICP Definition Engine

### Ideal Customer Profile

**Primary Decision-Maker Targets:**

| Title | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Head of Digital / Digital Strategy | Owns technology roadmap |
| Head of Visitor Experience | Owns the problem AITourPilot solves |
| Innovation Lead / Transformation Lead | Mandated to find new solutions |
| Director / Deputy Director | Decision authority at smaller institutions |
| Head of Education / Learning | Cares about accessibility + engagement |

**Museum Type (Sweet Spot):**

| Criterion | Ideal Range |
|-----------|-------------|
| Annual visitors | 50,000 - 2,000,000 |
| Digital maturity | Has app/audioguide OR actively seeking one |
| Open to innovation | Evidence of recent digital initiatives |
| Existing pain | Static audioguides, declining engagement, accessibility gaps |
| Region | DACH, Nordics, Benelux, Spain/Barcelona, France, UK |

**Tier 1 -- Highest Priority (Heritage Sites):**
- Large heritage sites (500K-5M visitors/year)
- UNESCO-listed or nationally significant
- Rich archives underutilized by current guides
- Examples: La Pedrera, Casa Batllo, Rijksmuseum, Uffizi, Prado, Acropolis Museum

**Tier 2 -- High Priority (Progressive Mid-Size):**
- 50K-500K visitors/year
- Active digital strategy
- Already invested in visitor experience innovation
- Examples: Moesgaard Museum (DK), Slot Loevestein (NL), Naturhistoriska (SE)

**Tier 3 -- Opportunistic (Small/Niche):**
- Strong thematic fit (war museums, design museums, science museums)
- May accept pilot pricing for case study value
- Examples: Staffordshire Regiment Museum (UK), Hunting Museum of Finland

### What Museums Actually Buy

Museums don't buy technology. They buy:

| What They Tell Themselves | What They Actually Need |
|--------------------------|------------------------|
| "We need better engagement" | Visitor dwell time increase (measurable) |
| "We want innovation" | Board-confidence through recognized technology |
| "We need accessibility" | 30+ languages without re-recording audioguides |
| "We need data" | Real-time operational intelligence (flow, sentiment, topics) |
| "We need funding justification" | Innovation reputation that attracts grants |

> Your messaging must shift from "AI art comes to life" to **"Your visitors will talk to Vermeer."** Subtle but massive difference.

## Module 3: Deep Personalization Engine

This is the competitive edge. Generic outreach gets 1-2% reply rates. Deep personalization gets 8-15%.

### For Each Target Museum, AI Generates:

1. **5 Insight Bullets** -- museum positioning, digital initiatives, current challenges
2. **1 Concrete Hypothesis** -- why AITourPilot specifically fits this museum
3. **1 Personalized Hook Line** -- opening sentence that proves you did your homework
4. **1 Tailored Conversation Starter** -- specific to their collection or recent news

### AI Research Prompt Structure

**Inputs per lead:**
- Museum website (key pages: about, exhibitions, visit info, press)
- Decision-maker's LinkedIn profile (recent posts, shared articles)
- Latest museum news / press releases
- Annual report if available
- Current audioguide solution (check app stores, TripAdvisor reviews)

**Output per lead:**
A personalized brief that feeds directly into Module 4 message templates.

### Example Output

> **Museum:** Rijksmuseum, Amsterdam
> **Contact:** Jan de Vries, Head of Digital
>
> **Insights:**
> 1. Recently launched AR experience for Rembrandt exhibition
> 2. Published digital strategy PDF emphasizing "accessibility for all"
> 3. Current audioguide via Smartify (image recognition, pre-recorded)
> 4. 2.7M visitors/year, 80% international -- multilingual is critical
> 5. TripAdvisor reviews mention "audioguide is outdated" (3.2/5 rating)
>
> **Hypothesis:** Rijksmuseum has invested heavily in digital but still serves pre-recorded audio. Their accessibility mandate + international audience makes them an ideal fit for real-time conversational AI in 30+ languages.
>
> **Hook:** "I noticed your digital strategy mentions making the collection accessible to every visitor regardless of language -- we've built something that delivers exactly that through real-time voice conversation."
>
> **Conversation starter:** "What would it look like if a visitor could ask Rembrandt why he painted himself so many times -- and get a thoughtful, historically grounded answer in their own language?"

### Cost Estimate

150 leads x ~2,000 tokens analysis = ~300,000 tokens. At GPT-4.1-mini rates, this costs approximately EUR 50-150 total.

### Demo Booking Funnel (Fully Automated)

The conversion path from campaign click to scheduled meeting is now frictionless:

```
LinkedIn ad / outreach email / organic post
        \u2193
Campaign landing page (engage.aitourpilot.com/museums)
        \u2193
"Book a 30-Minute Demo" button
        \u2193
Google Calendar appointment page (hermann@aitourpilot.com)
        \u2193
Automatic Google Meet link + calendar event + confirmation email
```

**Key design decisions:**
- **Personal founder email** (hermann@) signals founder-level attention for high-value B2B
- **30-minute slots** with 30-min descriptive format, Tue-Fri 10:00-15:30 CET
- **Custom booking fields** capture museum name + interest area for pre-call preparation
- **Secondary contact option** (info@aitourpilot.com) for lower-intent visitors who prefer email
- **No manual scheduling step** -- previously required email reply with meeting link, now instant

The main website (aitourpilot.com) serves as the brand hub with clear audience segmentation: museum visitors toward early access, museum professionals toward demo booking. Both paths converge on the campaign landing page.

### Why NOT Full Automation for LinkedIn

LinkedIn detects automated messages and restricts accounts. For 50-80 high-value contacts, manual LinkedIn touches with AI-drafted copy are more effective and safer than scraping + automation tools.

### Workflow

```
Day 0:  Email Message 1 (automated) + LinkedIn connection request (manual)
Day 3:  Email Message 2 (automated)
Day 5:  LinkedIn comment on their recent post (manual, if applicable)
Day 8:  Email Message 3 (automated)
Day 10: LinkedIn voice note or direct message (manual, high-touch)
```

## Module 6: Response Handling Engine

### Response Categories

| Score | Category | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| **5** | Meeting requested | Send booking link (Google Calendar), prepare personalized demo |
| **4** | Strong interest | Send La Pedrera-style proposal adapted to their museum |
| **3** | Interest, bad timing | Add to nurture sequence, follow up in 30/60/90 days |
| **2** | Unclear / forwarded | Research the new contact, restart Module 3 |
| **1** | Decline | Thank gracefully, add to long-term newsletter |

### AI-Generated Response Templates

For each category, pre-draft 2-3 response variants. Personalize before sending.

**Score 5 (Meeting):**
> "Wonderful -- here's the link to book a time directly: [Google Calendar booking link]. In the meantime, here's a 2-minute video showing AITourPilot in action at [relevant museum type]. Looking forward to our conversation."

**Score 3 (Later):**
> "Completely understand. I'll circle back in [timeframe]. In the meantime, would it be useful if I sent you a brief overview of what we learned from powering 28 museum guides? No commitment -- just insights from the space."
