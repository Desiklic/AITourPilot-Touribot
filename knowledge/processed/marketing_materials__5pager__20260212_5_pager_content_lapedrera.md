# PAGE 2: LA PEDRERA'S CHALLENGE

La Pedrera's challenge is not about adding technology. It is about activating something extraordinary that already exists.

**The knowledge gap.** La Pedrera possesses vast documentation -- architectural archives, scientific research, historical analysis, connections to buildings and archives across Barcelona. Decades of accumulated expertise. Today, visitors access almost none of it. Current audio guides deliver the same pre-established script to every visitor. An architecture student and a family with children get the same experience. The knowledge exists. The activation does not.

**The operational gap.** 3,300 visitors per day, and no real-time visibility into how they move through the building. Where congestion builds, how long people stay, whether the visit stays within its intended duration -- today this depends on staff observation, not data.

**The hardware burden.** Hundreds of physical devices to charge, distribute, collect, clean, maintain, and replace. Every day. La Pedrera is explicitly not looking for another hardware-dependent solution.

**The ambition.** What La Pedrera is seeking goes far beyond a better audio guide:

- Personalised content that adapts to each visitor's interests and language
- Interactive engagement where visitors ask questions and receive real-time answers
- Visitor behaviour data -- movement, dwell times, topics, preferences
- Pre-visit planning and post-visit follow-up
- Technology that supports human guides, not replaces them

This is the ISE 2026 Open Innovation Challenge. It maps precisely to what AITourPilot was built to deliver.

# PAGE 3: WHAT AITOURPILOT DELIVERS

AITourPilot is not an audio guide. It is a cultural intelligence platform built on three integrated layers.

## Layer 1: Knowledge Activation

**Your archives become conversations.**

AITourPilot ingests La Pedrera's documentation -- architectural research, scientific analysis, historical records, curatorial materials, and references from external archives -- and makes this knowledge accessible to every visitor through real-time, natural conversation. Visitors ask whatever they are curious about. The system responds with answers drawn from verified, curated institutional knowledge -- not from the open internet, not from generic AI models.

- **Adaptive to every visitor.** An architecture student and a family with children get fundamentally different experiences. The AI detects what each person is curious about and adapts depth, language, and approach in real time.
- **30+ languages, real-time.** Natural, fluent conversation in the visitor's own language -- including languages that would be economically impossible to offer through traditional audio guides.
- **Knowledge sovereignty.** La Pedrera's data stays La Pedrera's data. Trained exclusively on your content. Hosted on European servers. No data sharing. No cross-training. Full GDPR compliance.
- **Your team stays in control.** A content management system lets your team upload and manage content directly. We develop the AI's behaviour and personality together -- the tone, the boundaries, the guardrails -- so it represents La Pedrera exactly as you intend.

## Layer 2: Spatial Intelligence

**See your building breathe.**

Room-level visitor positioning using WiFi-based triangulation. No beacons. No Bluetooth tags. No hardware to install. The platform knows which room each visitor is in -- and so does the AI guide.

- **Real-time flow visibility.** Where visitors are at any moment. Where congestion builds. Dwell times per room and per exhibit.
- **Capacity management.** Visitor density against safe limits. Alerts when areas approach thresholds. Security and regulatory compliance supported by data, not estimation.
- **Intelligent nudging.** The AI naturally suggests less crowded areas or draws attention to something remarkable nearby. Flow management through conversation, not barriers.
- **Behavioural analytics.** Which rooms captivate visitors? What questions are asked most? Where do interest patterns emerge? Data that informs exhibition planning, staffing, and operations.

## Layer 3: Experience Personalisation

**Every visit is different because every visitor is different.**

- **Interest detection.** The system identifies what each visitor is drawn to -- architecture, history, science, daily life in 1912 -- and shapes the conversation around those interests.
- **Adaptive depth.** Some visitors want a brief overview. Others want twenty minutes on a single room. The AI calibrates based on engagement and questions.
- **Pre-visit and post-visit.** Visitors explore before arrival, plan their route. Afterwards, tailored follow-up deepens the connection and encourages return visits.
- **Accessibility.** Text mode for hearing-impaired visitors. Voice for those who prefer it. Every visitor gets full access to La Pedrera's knowledge.

# PAGE 4: YOUR QUESTIONS -- OUR ANSWERS

During our meeting on February 12, 2026, several important questions were raised. Here are our direct responses.

## "How do you support visitors who don't want to interact with AI -- like elderly visitors who prefer a fixed tour?"

Not every visitor wants a conversation. Some prefer a traditional guided experience -- and that is completely valid.

AITourPilot supports **pre-made, structured tours** alongside the conversational mode. These are curated routes through the building with prepared content that plays at each location -- similar to a traditional audio guide but drawing from the full depth of La Pedrera's archives.

These fixed tours use the same positioning technology. The system knows where the visitor is and delivers the right content at the right moment -- without requiring the visitor to interact, ask questions, or navigate an interface.

Both modes -- conversational and guided -- run on the same platform, the same visitor device, and use the same institutional knowledge base. The visitor chooses their preferred experience at the start.

## "What about reliability? You're running 24/7 with hundreds of visitors every day."

This is non-negotiable for us. La Pedrera cannot stop receiving visitors because of a technical problem.

**Our approach to reliability:**

- **Industry-leading technology partners.** We work with the best voice AI and language model providers in the industry. We choose partners for reliability and quality, not for cost optimisation. For a premium museum experience, this matters.
- **SLA-backed support.** We structure support agreements with guaranteed response times that match La Pedrera's operational reality. This includes remote monitoring, proactive issue detection, and rapid escalation paths.
- **Graceful degradation.** If any component experiences issues, the system falls back gracefully. Cached content continues. Static backup activates. Visitors never see an error screen or a broken experience.
- **Cloud architecture.** The platform runs on enterprise-grade cloud infrastructure with redundancy built in. No single point of failure. No dependency on on-site hardware.

We built this for institutions that cannot afford downtime. That is the standard we hold ourselves to.

## "What about group tours -- a group listening to the same AI together?"

This is an excellent use case. A guided group that can collectively interact with an AI guide -- asking questions as a group, with everyone hearing the same response.

We have this capability in our development roadmap and see the La Pedrera co-development as the ideal context to build it together. The concept:

- **Group mode:** One AI guide serves the whole group. Any group member can ask a question. Everyone hears the answer.
- **Seamless switch:** At any point, individual visitors can detach from the group and continue their own personal conversation with the AI.
- **Guide companion mode:** The human guide leads, but the AI serves as a real-time knowledge companion -- helping the guide answer questions that go beyond their prepared content.

This feature speaks directly to how La Pedrera currently operates: guided tours are central to your offering, and technology should strengthen the guide's role rather than compete with it.

## "Can we upload our own content? Do we have a CMS?"

Yes. La Pedrera's team will have a content management system to upload, organise, and update the knowledge base that powers the AI.

But content management alone is not enough. Equally important is the **AI behaviour layer** -- how the system uses that content. This includes:

- **The system prompt:** The personality, tone, and style of the AI guide. Should it be formal or conversational? Should it proactively offer information or wait to be asked? How should it handle topics outside its knowledge?
- **Guardrails:** What the AI should never say. What topics are off-limits. How it handles misinformation or inappropriate questions.
- **Content priorities:** Which archives and sources take precedence. How conflicts between sources are resolved. What level of detail is appropriate for different visitor segments.

We develop this together. Your team's institutional expertise shapes the AI's behaviour. Our technical expertise ensures it works reliably and naturally. The result is an AI guide that represents La Pedrera authentically -- because it was built with La Pedrera.

# PAGE 5: ISE CHALLENGE REQUIREMENTS -- DIRECT MAPPING

The ISE 2026 Open Innovation Challenge defines specific requirements. Here is how AITourPilot addresses each one.

**Personalised, interactive content with multiple language support**
Every conversation is unique. The AI adapts to individual interests and background in real time. 30+ languages through natural conversation -- not pre-recorded scripts. Answers drawn from La Pedrera's own verified documentation.

**Compatible with institution devices and visitor smartphones**
BYOD-first. Visitors use their own phones. Also compatible with institution-provided devices where needed (accessibility, loaner phones). No app download required for core functionality.

**Collect and analyse data on visitor behaviour**
Room-level positioning provides movement data, dwell times, and capacity monitoring. The conversational layer provides topic analytics -- what visitors ask, what engages them most, where interest patterns emerge. Physical and intellectual visitor behaviour, combined.

**Pre-visit planning and post-visit follow-up**
Visitors explore before arrival -- areas of interest, route planning. After the visit, personalised follow-up deepens the connection: topics they explored, related recommendations, prompts that encourage return visits.

**NOT conventional static audio/video guides**
No fixed script. No pre-recorded content. Every interaction generated in real time, shaped by the visitor's questions, in their own language. The difference between reading a placard and having a conversation with someone who genuinely knows the building.
