## 2. Comparative Analysis

### Side-by-Side Comparison

| Metric | T1 English | T1 German | T2 Van Gogh |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Reach | 5,999 | 4,589 | 10,395 |
| CPM | EUR 13.99 | EUR 21.58 | **EUR 9.62** |
| CTR | **1.25%** | 1.08% | 0.65% |
| CPC | **EUR 1.12** | EUR 2.00 | EUR 1.47 |
| Engagement Rate | **1.34%** | 1.19% | 0.71% |
| View Rate | **28.9%** | 27.3% | 25.47% |
| CPV | EUR 0.05 | EUR 0.08 | **EUR 0.04** |
| Completion Rate | 0.62% | 0.65% | **2.75%** |
| Landing Page Clicks | **9** | 6 | 2 |

### Key Takeaways

1. **Van Gogh (T2) won on reach efficiency** — nearly 2x the reach at the lowest CPM (EUR 9.62 vs 13.99/21.58), likely due to Audience Expansion being enabled
2. **Ninth Wave (T1 EN) won on engagement quality** — highest CTR (1.25%), best engagement rate (1.34%), most landing page clicks (9)
3. **Van Gogh had dramatically better video completion** — 2.75% vs ~0.63%, driven by its shorter format (12s vs 30s)
4. **German campaign underperformed across all metrics** — higher costs, lower engagement, zero LinkedIn page clicks
5. **Longer dwell time on German** (3.28s) suggests engaged viewers stayed longer but were fewer in number
