## 1. What This Museum Is Telling You (Between the Lines)

From the transcript, they reveal **five key signals**:

---

### A. They Are Mission-Driven, Not Revenue-Maximizers

> “Visitors allow us to fund social, environmental, health programmes…”

This is not a “let’s monetize visitors harder” institution.
They see visitors as **means to fund a foundation mission**.

➡️ They will resist:

* Aggressive per-user monetization
* Heavy revenue sharing
* “Let’s squeeze more € per visitor”

They prefer **stable, ethical, predictable models**.

---

### B. Their Core Pain Is: Knowledge Unlocking

They repeat:

> “We have plenty of documentation… archives… libraries… scientific info…”

They are sitting on a **huge intellectual asset** they can’t operationalize.

➡️ You are not “selling an app”.
You are selling:

> “We unlock your institutional knowledge.”

This favors **project-based + platform models**, not pure SaaS.

---

### C. They Want Operations + Analytics + Crowd Control

They care about:

* Where visitors are
* How long they stay
* Flow control
* Security
* Capacity

This is **operations software**, not just content.

➡️ Operations software is almost never revenue-share.
It’s licensed.

---

### D. They Hate Hardware & Complexity

> “We are not thinking in physical devices…”

They want BYOD + simple ops.

➡️ This points to:

* Centralized platform
* Long-term vendor relationship
* Not “pay-per-use”

---

### E. They Care Deeply About Support & Reliability

> “We cannot stop… we need technical service very close…”

This is critical.

They are buying:

* SLA
* Partner
* Vendor stability

Not “cool startup experiment”.

➡️ This supports **subscription + support contract**, not transactional models.

## 2. What This Means: Which Models Will NOT Work Well

For *this* museum, avoid as primary model:

### ❌ Pure Revenue Share (50/50 etc.)

Why:

* They don’t frame success in ticket revenue
* They don’t mention monetization at all
* They’re publicly accountable

They’ll see this as:

> “Why should we give away income from a public asset?”

---

### ❌ Pure Pay-Per-Visitor

Why:

* Forecasting nightmare
* Finance departments hate it
* “Success tax” fear

They’ll ask:

> “What if it explodes in August?”

---

### ❌ Freemium / Ad / Visitor Pays Directly

Not aligned with their mission.
Likely politically sensitive.
