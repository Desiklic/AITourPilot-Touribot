# Email Outreach Templates — 3-Step Sequence

*Loaded when drafting emails. Each message serves a distinct psychological purpose.*

## Message 1: Insight Hook (Day 0)

**Purpose:** Demonstrate homework. Create curiosity.

**Structure:**
1. Specific observation about their museum
2. Relevance to a challenge they face
3. Micro-CTA (not "buy" — just "exchange")

**Template:**
> Subject: [Museum Name] + conversational AI — a thought
>
> Hi [First Name],
>
> [Personalized hook — specific detail about their museum].
>
> We've built AITourPilot — a conversational voice AI that turns museum archives into real-time dialogues. Visitors ask questions and get thoughtful, historically grounded answers in their own language. No screens, no QR codes — just eyes on the art and a voice that knows the building.
>
> We recently won "Best Emerging Cultural AI Experience 2026" in Spain and currently power 28 museum guides across 5 languages.
>
> Would a 30-minute demo be interesting? You can book directly here: [booking link]
>
> Or just reply "yes" and I'll send details.
>
> Best regards,
> Hermann Kudlich
> Co-Founder, AITourPilot

## Message 2: Social Proof + Hypothesis (Day 3-5)

**Purpose:** Build credibility. Show understanding of their specific situation.

**Template:**
> Hi [First Name],
>
> Quick follow-up — I had a specific hypothesis about why [Museum Name] might benefit from what we're building:
>
> [Hypothesis — specific to their museum, from research]
>
> For context: our content factory can transform a museum's existing documentation into a live AI voice guide in under 24 hours. We currently serve 28 museums and were recently recognized at the Spanish Business Awards.
>
> Would this be worth a 30-minute conversation? Book a slot directly: [booking link]
>
> Best,
> Hermann

## Message 3: Pattern Interrupt (Day 8-10)

**Purpose:** Break silence with a different approach. Low-pressure.

**Template:**
> Hi [First Name],
>
> I know museum professionals are busy — especially this time of year.
>
> Quick question: is visitor engagement innovation a strategic priority for [Museum Name] in 2026, or is the focus elsewhere right now?
>
> Either way, happy to share what we're learning from working with 28 museums across Europe. Just reply "yes" or "not now" — either works.
>
> Best,
> Hermann

## Drafting Rules (always apply)

1. Under 200 words for initial outreach, under 150 for follow-ups
2. Opens with something specific — never a generic compliment
3. One clear ask — never multiple CTAs
4. No superlatives ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "world-class" are banned)
5. Ends with a specific, low-friction question
6. Language matches the contact: German for DACH, French for France/Switzerland, Dutch for NL
7. Never make up facts about a museum — say what you don't know
