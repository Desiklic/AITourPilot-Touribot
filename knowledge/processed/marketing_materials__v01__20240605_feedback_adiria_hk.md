Feedback 
Website

Header:

more elegant but conservative than the current online version. Fonts and brush stroke are beautiful, but in my opinion has less emotional impact and creates less attention.

About IA Tour Pilot
[Acerca de IA Tour Pilot]:

This is a similar text than the 'Who we are' from the deck so I’ll not rewrite my comments.

The last sentence ' Will you join the new art revolution?' might be a too bold statement in the sense of that museum decision makers won't believe it (yet). With this we are very much in their territory and the judgement of this is subjective.

Unique Experiences & Interactivity and Personalization

the text are quite basic, not special enough and sound like 'heard before'. Sentences like ' innovative experiences, outside the norm' or 'making you discover and enjoy art like never before'.

Explore the world with our guIA

I'd leave out 'no tricks' - don't think this is a sales argument but makes people think about tricks. We want them to think of (and focus on) something else.

I'd leave out the 'initial questionnaire', which sounds like 'work' and at the same time does not provide value to the user.

I'd leave out: 'We want to know who you are, what your tastes and interests are. With a few simple questions, we'll set everything up to build a unique museum experience.' In today’s world this is a big issue and will make people think about privacy and perhaps raises some alarm bells, especially because the usage is not entire clear, and the value might not be perceived as high enough at this point. We need to create a desire for the product and trust first.

This section should razor sharp bring the experience to live in words, create interest and curiosity. Colourfully tell what transformative experience they will get into when using the app in a museum visit. 
This text is going in this direction, but it is not engaging enough: 'Throughout the tour, we will guide you, tell you anecdotes, interesting facts, and recommend something to see. Above all, we want you to discover, become passionate, and have fun.'

No need to tell 'Download app, it's free and easy' - this should be experienced on downloading.

Are you a museum?

We are not just any AI: 
We index all the museum's documentation, understand the visitor's tastes with a few brief questions, mix it all up, and create a tailored tour.

Good title and good direction 'indexing ...' - but understanding visitor's tastes with brief questions does not cater for the complexity we undergo to personalize. There is much more than just asking questions (which we might skip as much as possible).

The text is generally too simple, not 'catchy' enough (few questions, mixing it up, tailored tour) - sounds so simple that interest might fade.

We create new tour flows:

this was better written in the deck ('Mona Lisa effect ...')

Valuable data:

'Considering relocating a piece will be our greatest achievement.' might be an argument that is not well received. Also, it might not be the strongest argument and creating desires to work with us.

Having the data point as topic/argument is good though
